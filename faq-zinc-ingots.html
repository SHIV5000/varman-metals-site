What is the difference between zinc and galvanized steel?

Zinc Ingot

Pure zinc metal in solid form (99.9%+ purity)

Galvanized Steel

Steel coated with zinc layer for corrosion protection

Key Difference: Zinc ingots are the raw material used to create galvanized steel through hot-dip galvanizing process.

How long will zinc plating last outside?

Zinc plating longevity depends on:

Thickness

Hot-dip: 1.0 mil (50+ years) • Electroplated: 0.2 mil (10-20 years)

Environment

Rural: 50-100 years • Coastal: 20-50 years • Industrial: 20-40 years

Maintenance

Regular inspection extends lifespan significantly

Note: Zinc acts as sacrificial anode, corroding before the base steel rusts.

Which is better: galvanized or zinc plated?

Hot-Dip Galvanizing

  • Thicker coating (1.0 mil)
  • Longer lifespan (50+ years)
  • Better outdoor protection
  • Higher cost
  • Less precise coating

Zinc Electroplating

  • Thinner coating (0.2 mil)
  • More precise application
  • Better for intricate parts
  • Shorter lifespan (10-20 years)
  • Less corrosion resistance

Selection depends on: Application, environment, budget, and required lifespan.
