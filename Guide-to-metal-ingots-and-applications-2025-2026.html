Aluminium Ingots

Overview

Aluminium is the most abundant metal in the Earth's crust, known for its light weight, corrosion resistance, and excellent conductivity. Aluminium ingots are produced through electrolytic reduction of alumina or recycling of aluminium scrap.

India ranks as the second largest aluminium producer in the world, with major producers including Hindalco Industries and Vedanta Limited.

Key Properties

  • Density: 2.7 g/cm³ (about 1/3 of steel)
  • Melting Point: 660°C
  • Excellent electrical and thermal conductivity
  • Highly corrosion resistant
  • Non-toxic and recyclable

Zinc Ingots

Overview

Zinc is a bluish-white metal known for its anti-corrosion properties. It's the fourth most widely used metal after iron, aluminium, and copper. Approximately 50% of zinc production is used for galvanizing steel.

India is a significant producer and consumer of zinc, with Hindustan Zinc Limited being one of the world's largest integrated zinc producers.

Key Properties

  • Density: 7.14 g/cm³
  • Melting Point: 419.5°C
  • Excellent corrosion resistance
  • Forms protective zinc carbonate layer when exposed to air
  • Essential micronutrient for humans and crops

Lead Ingots

Overview

Lead is a dense, malleable, and corrosion-resistant metal with a low melting point. It has been used by humans for thousands of years. Modern applications are dominated by lead-acid batteries, which account for about 80% of global lead consumption.

India has a significant lead recycling industry, with companies like Gravita India leading in secondary lead production.

Key Properties

  • Density: 11.34 g/cm³ (very heavy)
  • Melting Point: 327.5°C (relatively low)
  • Excellent corrosion resistance
  • High density provides effective radiation shielding
  • Malleable and ductile

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about metal ingots, their properties, applications, and global trade.

What are metal ingots and what are they used for?

Metal ingots are standardized blocks of pure metal or alloy produced by melting and casting raw materials into molds. They serve as primary raw materials for manufacturing industries including automotive, construction, packaging, electronics, and infrastructure. Ingots provide consistent composition and quality, making them ideal for further processing into sheets, rods, wires, and cast components.

What is the difference between primary and secondary aluminium ingots?

Primary aluminium ingots are produced directly from bauxite ore through the electrolytic Hall-Héroult process, typically with purity levels of 99.7% or higher. Secondary aluminium ingots are produced by recycling aluminium scrap, offering cost advantages while maintaining good quality for many applications. Primary ingots have more consistent composition, while secondary ingots are more environmentally sustainable.

Why is high purity zinc (99.99%) required for galvanizing?

High purity zinc (99.99% or Special High Grade) is essential for galvanizing because impurities like lead, cadmium, and iron can negatively affect the galvanizing process. Impurities can cause rough, uneven coatings with poor adhesion, reducing corrosion protection. High-purity zinc ensures smooth, uniform coatings with excellent corrosion resistance and aesthetic appearance.

What are the main applications of lead ingots?

Lead ingots are primarily used in lead-acid batteries (about 80% of consumption), which power automobiles, UPS systems, and renewable energy storage. Other applications include radiation shielding in medical and nuclear facilities, ammunition, weights, solders, and specialized alloys. Lead's high density, corrosion resistance, and low melting point make it suitable for these applications.

How does the London Metal Exchange (LME) affect ingot prices?

The London Metal Exchange (LME) is the world's largest market for industrial metals, setting global benchmark prices for aluminium, zinc, lead, and other metals. LME prices are influenced by supply-demand dynamics, geopolitical factors, currency fluctuations, and economic indicators. Local ingot prices typically track LME prices plus regional premiums, transportation costs, taxes, and local market conditions.

What is the difference between ADC12 and LM24 aluminium alloys?

ADC12 and LM24 are both aluminium-silicon casting alloys, but with different compositions and properties. ADC12 has higher silicon content (10.5-12%) providing excellent fluidity and castability for thin-walled components. LM24 has lower silicon (7.5-9.5%) but higher copper content (3-4%), offering better mechanical strength and hardness. ADC12 is preferred for complex castings, while LM24 is used for components requiring higher strength.

Which countries are the largest importers of metal ingots?

The largest importers of metal ingots vary by metal type. For aluminium, major importers include United States, Germany, Japan, South Korea, and Turkey. For zinc, top importers are United States, Germany, Netherlands, South Korea, and Belgium. For lead, major import markets include United States, South Korea, Germany, Spain, and India. These countries typically have strong manufacturing sectors but limited domestic production.

What certifications are important for metal ingot quality?

Important certifications for metal ingots include BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards) certification for the Indian market, ISO 9001 for quality management systems, SGS inspection certificates for international trade, REACH compliance for European markets, and mill test certificates providing detailed chemical analysis. For specific applications, industry-specific certifications like automotive (IATF 16949) or aerospace (AS9100) may be required.

How are metal ingots transported internationally?

Metal ingots are typically transported in shipping containers via sea freight for international trade. They are palletized and securely stacked to maximize container space. For urgent or smaller shipments, air freight may be used. Proper packaging with moisture barriers and clear labeling is essential. Documentation includes commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading, certificate of origin, and insurance certificates.

What factors affect the price of metal ingots?

Metal ingot prices are influenced by multiple factors including LME benchmark prices, supply-demand balance, production costs (energy, labor, raw materials), currency exchange rates, geopolitical events, trade policies and tariffs, transportation costs, quality specifications, and market speculation. Regional factors like local taxes, import duties, and domestic production capacity also affect final prices to consumers.

Is it better to buy primary or secondary aluminium ingots?

Primary aluminium ingots (from raw bauxite) offer higher purity (99.7%+) and consistency, ideal for critical applications. Secondary ingots (from recycled scrap) are more cost-effective and environmentally friendly, suitable for many general applications. Choice depends on your quality requirements and budget.

What is the difference between virgin and remelted aluminium ingots?

Virgin aluminium is produced directly from bauxite ore through electrolysis, offering highest purity. Remelted aluminium is produced by recycling scrap metal, which may contain trace impurities but is more sustainable and cost-effective. Virgin aluminium has more consistent composition for critical applications.

What is aluminium ADC12 and where is it used?

ADC12 is an aluminium-silicon alloy (10.5-12% silicon) specifically designed for die-casting applications. It offers excellent fluidity, pressure tightness, and good mechanical properties. Widely used in automotive parts, engine components, electronics housings, and complex-shaped industrial components requiring precision casting.

What is LM24 aluminium alloy?

LM24 is an aluminium alloy with 7.5-9.5% silicon and 3-4% copper content. It provides better mechanical strength and hardness compared to ADC12, making it suitable for applications requiring higher strength like engine blocks, transmission cases, and heavy-duty automotive components.

What does 99.7% purity mean in aluminium ingots?

99.7% purity means the aluminium contains 99.7% pure aluminium with maximum 0.3% total impurities (mainly iron and silicon). This high purity ensures excellent conductivity, corrosion resistance, and workability, making it ideal for electrical applications, high-quality castings, and specialized industrial uses.

Difference: SHG zinc vs HG vs PW (trader quick check)?

SHG is 99.995% purity, HG is ~99.95%, and Prime Western ~99.5%; galvanizers generally prefer SHG for coating quality, while cost-sensitive or noncritical uses may accept HG or PW based on coating performance needs and price gaps.

Primary vs secondary aluminium price spread drivers India?

The spread reflects purity consistency, brand premium, recycling feed availability, power costs, and demand from die casters vs extruders; secondary tightens to primary in scarcity, and widens when scrap inflows and runs are healthy.

ADC12 chemical composition shortcut for buyers (key ranges)?

Expect Si ~10.5–12%, Cu ~1.5–3.5% with controlled Fe, Mn, Zn, Mg; verify MTCs show ranges within standard tolerances and check fluidity and pressure tightness tests if sourcing for thin-wall or leak-critical die castings at scale.

LM24 vs ADC12 selection for automotive die casting India vendors?

ADC12 offers superior castability for complex, thin sections; LM24, with higher Cu and lower Si, gives better mechanical strength and hardness; choose ADC12 for geometry-driven casting yield, LM24 for strength-critical housings or covers with robust wall stock and machining allowance plans.

Meaning of 99.7 aluminium purity on invoice/MTC for conductors?

It denotes 99.7% minimum Al, with total impurities capped near 0.3% (primarily Fe+Si); this grade supports high electrical conductivity and corrosion resistance, suitable for bus bars, conductors, and demanding extrusion applications with tight properties.

Zinc ingot for hot-dip galvanizing—why insist on SHG 99.995?

Trace impurities like Pb, Cd, and Fe can cause coating roughness, drips, and poor adhesion; SHG minimizes such defects, delivering smooth, uniform, and more durable coatings, reducing rework and improving line throughput metrics for galvanizers under tight specs.

MTC (Mill Test Certificate) must-have fields for ingot procurement?

Include heat/lot number, grade, chemical composition with tolerances, purity, weight, number of pieces, standard referenced (ASTM/EN/BIS), date, and QA sign-off; for exports, align with buyer’s spec and add third-party inspection if required by LC terms or QA plans.

Difference between brand premium and regional premium in ingots?

Brand premium reflects producer reputation, consistency, and acceptance in end-user audits; regional premium covers location-based logistics, port congestion, and local inventory costs; both add on top of LME basis when pricing spot or contract deliveries domestically or CFR/FOB internationally.

Packing best practices for containerized ingot shipments (humidity, shift)?

Use strapped bundles on treated pallets, use corner protectors, apply moisture barriers/desiccants in humid seasons, maintain center of gravity low with even stacking, and stencil clear markings; photograph stuffing and seal for claim evidence and ensure weight distribution meets container axle load norms and line rules.

Factor expected melt loss 1–3% for clean primary and higher for secondary or contaminated charge, plus flux and dross handling; effective metal cost per finished kg must include yield, cycle gas/power, and scrap return value, not just the invoice ingot rate, to quote profitably in competitive die casting contracts.

Zinc ingot storage best practices for galvanizers (avoid white rust and contamination)?

Keep indoors on pallets, avoid water contact, use breathable covers to reduce condensation, practice FIFO by heat/lot, and prevent cross-contamination with steel dust or chemicals; inspect bundles on receipt and note any oxidation or strap damage in the LR for timely claims or supplier resolution before furnace charging begins that week.

Lead ingot handling PPE and precautions in battery plants and warehouses (safety quick-list)?

Use gloves, masks/respirators where dust or fumes may occur, maintain ventilation near melting, enforce hygiene (no eating in handling areas), and monitor blood lead levels for exposed staff as per local regulations; segregate storage, manage dross safely, and document training to satisfy audits and legal compliance for hazardous metal operations on site.

LME warrants vs physical premiums—why domestic India price deviates from LME math some days?

LME reflects warehouse-deliverable liquidity, while Indian spot includes regional premiums, rail/road capacity, festival slowdowns, port congestion, and local supply-demand mismatches; therefore, even with stable LME, domestic price can rise if trucks or containers are scarce or a large buyer pulls forward monthly allocations suddenly in your region.

Tolerance on bundle weights—how do buyers handle short or overage on receipt of ingots?
